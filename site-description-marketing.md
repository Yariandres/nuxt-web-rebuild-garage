# Web Rebuild Garage — Marketing & Sales Description

> How we talk about the offer, who it’s for, and how we turn visitors into booked calls — without the build details.

---

## One-line pitch

**We’re the pit crew for websites that are bleeding money, speed, or trust** — not another agency deck. You get a short diagnosis, a focused fix window, and optional ongoing cover.

---

## Concept (brand story)

| Idea | What it means for the buyer |
| ---- | --------------------------- |
| **Website = wrecked supercar** | The problem feels expensive, embarrassing, and urgent — but it’s fixable with the right crew. |
| **Pit crew, not agency** | Small team, hands-on, no layers. You talk to the people who do the work. |
| **Episodes = proof** | Real fixes, told like a rebuild show: before → what was wrong → after. Trust through transparency, not credentials alone. |

---

## Who we’re for

**Best fit**

- Stores or sites where **checkout, speed, or reliability** directly hit revenue or reputation.
- Teams who’ve already tried plugins, freelancers, or “optimization” and still see **drop-offs, errors, or scary metrics**.
- Decision-makers who want **clarity first** (what’s actually broken?) before a big retainer.

**Not a fit (say so early)**

- Greenfield “build my site from zero” as the main ask (unless paired with a clear rescue scope).
- Pure strategy/branding with no technical problem to solve.
- Buyers who need a 50-person agency matrix; we sell **speed and ownership**, not headcount.

---

## Problems we own (buyer language)

Frame these as **symptoms they feel**, not stack names:

| They say… | We translate to… |
| --------- | ----------------- |
| “Checkout feels stuck; people abandon.” | Revenue leak at the moment of truth. |
| “Google/tools flag our site as slow.” | Search and ads punished; first impression damaged. |
| “Things randomly break after updates.” | Trust and support load spiral; brand looks fragile. |
| “The back office is a slog.” | Team time lost; errors slip through. |
| “We fear traffic spikes or deploys.” | Growth capped by fear of downtime or performance cliffs. |

**Routing**: Each pain point should map to a clear **entry story** on the site (landing snippets, episode tags, booking prefill) so prospects feel “this is exactly us.”

## Offer ladder (how we package and sell it)

| Step | Name | What they buy | Outcome we promise |
| ---- | ---- | ------------- | ------------------- |
| 1 | **Rapid Diagnosis** | One focused session (teardown call). | Named problems, priority order, and honest “fix vs. replace” — **no mystery quote**. |
| 2 | **Rescue Sprint** | A short, bounded engagement. | Measurable improvement on the agreed pain (speed, checkout, stability, etc.). |
| 3 | **Pit Crew Retainer** | Ongoing monitoring and response. | Peace of mind after a win; we catch regressions before customers do. |

**Sales note**: Diagnosis is the **primary CTA everywhere** — low commitment, high clarity, natural upsell to sprint.

---

## Differentiators (messaging pillars)

1. **Diagnosis before dollars** — We sell understanding first; scope follows evidence.
2. **Show the work** — Episodes and before/after metrics replace vague “we’re experts.”
3. **Two-person crew, clear roles** — Lead web diagnosis + delivery; infrastructure and scale when the problem lives there.
4. **Garage tone** — Direct, mechanical language (teardown, rebuild, test drive, work order) instead of corporate fluff.

---

## Voice & visual vibe (for copy and ads)

- **Sound like a workshop**, not a boardroom: short sentences, concrete verbs, numbers where possible.
- **Borrow the rebuild-show rhythm**: hook → mess → method → payoff.
- **Proof blocks**: before/after metrics, quote-worthy results, episode titles that read like episode names, not blog SEO slugs.

---

## Content engine: Episodes (marketing role)

**Why episodes exist**

- **Trust at scale**: Prospects see their pain reflected in a real story.
- **SEO and shareability**: Each episode is a long-tail answer to “slow checkout,” “vitals failing,” etc.
- **Sales enablement**: Send one link before a call — *“This is similar to what we did for X.”*

**What each episode should answer for a buyer**

1. What did it *feel* like before? (symptom)
2. What was actually wrong? (plain English)
3. What did we do? (high level — no need for tool laundry lists in customer-facing copy)
4. What changed? (metrics, behavior, money, time)

## Conversion system

- **Primary action**: Book **Rapid Diagnosis** (same label everywhere for consistency).
- **Context passing**: Booking links can carry symptom/source so the first call starts warm.
- **Secondary actions**: Watch an episode, read a case metric, “email us” only if booking isn’t possible.

---

## Objections to preempt (FAQ and sales)

| Objection | Response angle |
| --------- | --------------- |
| “We already have a dev.” | We’re surgical on performance/reliability; we can align with your team or own a slice. |
| “How do I know it’s worth it?” | Diagnosis is bounded; sprint scope ties to agreed metrics. |
| “Will you disappear after?” | Retainer is optional; episodes show ongoing relationships where it made sense. |
| “Agencies burned us.” | Pit crew model: named people, visible work, no black box. |

---

## Metrics that sell (examples)

Use **before → after** everywhere it’s honest:

- Checkout or key flow time
- Core user-facing signals (speed, error rate) in language the buyer understands
- Business proxies: fewer support tickets, recovered conversion — when you can claim them
